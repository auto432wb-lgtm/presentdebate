Pre-Columbus Evidence (Negative)

Intro Round

There is not yet sufficient high-standard, peer-verified evidence to confirm sustained pre-Columbian Black African presence in the Americas as established fact.

Premise Round

  • Burden of proof: Extraordinary historical claims require strong, replicable evidence.
  • Method standards: Iconography/speculation is not equal to datable archaeology/genetics.
  • Consensus: Current mainstream scholarship does not treat this as confirmed history.

Rebuttal Round

  • “Oral history proves it” → Valuable context, but not conclusive without corroborating physical evidence.
  • “Consensus is biased” → Possible bias doesn’t remove evidentiary standards.

Rapid Fire

  • Name one uncontested peer-reviewed site proving pre-1492 Black African presence.
  • Are you arguing possibility or proof?
  • Do all historical claims get equal evidence standards?
