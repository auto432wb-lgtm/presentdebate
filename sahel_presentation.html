06 — The Blueprint

Sankara's
Prophecy

"He who feeds you, controls you. Refuse to be fed the narrative that your poverty is inevitable. The resources beneath your feet belong to your children."

Thomas Sankara governed Burkina Faso from 1983 to 1987. In four years: he refused IMF debt, nationalized resources, launched a reforestation program planting millions of trees, achieved food self-sufficiency, banned female genital mutilation, built schools and roads with local labor. Infant mortality fell. Literacy rose.

On October 15, 1987 — he was assassinated. His successor Blaise Compaoré, backed by France and Côte d'Ivoire, reversed every policy within months. The IMF returned.

"The West calls these men dictators. But the dictator who let France take the gold — he was just called a president."
