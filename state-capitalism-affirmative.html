US State-Sponsored Capitalism (Affirmative)

Intro Round

The U.S. is capitalist, and that capitalism is materially state-sponsored through policy, subsidies, procurement, and monetary intervention.

Premise Round

  • Policy architecture: Tax code, subsidies, and regulation shape winners/losers.
  • Monetary backstop: Crisis interventions stabilize major capital sectors.
  • Federal contracting: Defense/public spending drives core industries.

Rebuttal Round

  • “That’s just governance” → At U.S. scale and selectivity, it functions as sponsorship.
  • “Free markets can’t be sponsored” → Mixed systems can be both market and state-shaped.

Rapid Fire

  • Do bailouts/subsidies materially alter market outcomes or not?
  • Would key sectors look the same without federal contracts?
  • Are you debating theory or actual U.S. practice?
