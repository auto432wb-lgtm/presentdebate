The Original Directive

The Five Tactics

01

Infiltration

Agents and informers didn't merely spy on political activists but were used to discredit and disrupt movements.

02

Negative Public Image

Creating a negative public image for target groups by releasing negative personal information.

03

Internal Conflict

Breaking down internal organization by creating conflicts — exacerbating racial tensions, sending anonymous letters.

04

Dissension Between Groups

Spreading rumors that other groups were stealing money. FBI directed field offices to "exploit all avenues of creating further dissension."

05

Restrict Resources

Pressuring non-profit organizations to cut off funding or material support to target groups.

Who Is John Tanton?

Tanton was the racist architect of the modern anti-immigrant movement. He had white nationalist beliefs and wrote that to maintain American culture, "a European-American majority" is required.[56]

"I've come to the point of view that for European-American society and culture to persist requires a European-American majority, and a clear one at that."

— JOHN TANTON, DECEMBER 10, 1993

FAIR received more than $1.2 million from the Pioneer Fund, a racist foundation devoted to proving a connection between race and intelligence.[79]

THE SOCIAL CONTRACT PRESS

Tanton ran this racist publishing company. One special issue was devoted to "Europhobia: The Hostility Toward European-Descended Americans."[56]

THE CAMP OF THE SAINTS

In 1994, Tanton republished this racist French novel describing "swarthy hordes" of immigrants. He called it "prescient."[56]

NETWORK REACH

Founded FAIR, Center for Immigration Studies (CIS), and NumbersUSA — profoundly shaping the immigration debate.[56]

Black Identity Extremists

COINTELPRO operations officially ended in the 1970s. But its legacy is alive and well. In 2017, a leaked FBI report defined the security threat posed by "Black Identity Extremists" — a name that for many echoes the inflammatory labels given to civil rights groups during the COINTELPRO era.
